\## act\_1







\### CHAPTER 01



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------



\#### SCENE PLAN FOR CHAPTER 1



\#### POV: Thea



\#### POV CHARACTER EXPERIENCE:



Thea arrives at the Chicago Ballet Company theater with Carolina to investigate a body. The victim—a woman named Astrid—triggers overwhelming grief in Thea despite never having met her. Something about this stranger feels devastatingly familiar. The crime scene defies logic: frozen roses spiral around the body in Chicago's brutal summer heat. Against all her training and Carolina's protests, Thea feels compelled to touch the roses barehanded. The moment she makes contact, a violent vision overtakes her—she's suddenly seeing through someone else's eyes, feeling their satisfaction and desperate purpose as they hunt this woman. She glimpses a face reflected in a theater window, but a crushing migraine tears her back to reality. The face immediately blurs from memory, leaving only bone-deep dread. Carolina scolds her for contaminating evidence while Thea struggles with terror that she's losing her mind.



\#### 1\\. THE CHANGE (What shifts for the POV character):



Thea experiences what she believes is a psychotic break—a vivid hallucination that felt more real than reality itself, giving her impossible knowledge while making her question her own sanity.



\#### 2\\. WHY IT MATTERS (To the POV character):



This "hallucination" provides Thea with specific investigative details she couldn't possibly know (the killer's hunting behavior, his emotional investment, his methods) while simultaneously making her fear for her mental stability. She must choose between dismissing the vision as mental illness or accepting that she somehow witnessed the murder through impossible means. Her decision to pursue what she saw, despite fearing for her sanity, becomes the only way to prove she's not losing her mind.



\#### 4\\. THE SCENE:



\* \*\*Opens:\*\* Thea stepping into impossible cold—frozen roses in Chicago's brutal summer heat

\* \*\*Location:\*\* Chicago Ballet Company theater, backstage area where the body lies

\* \*\*Time:\*\* Early morning, approximately 6 AM

\* \*\*Key Moments (as Thea experiences them):\*\*



&nbsp; \* Overwhelming grief upon seeing Astrid—like losing someone beloved

&nbsp; \* Compulsion to touch evidence despite years of protocol training

&nbsp; \* Vision: riding inside someone else's mind, feeling their hunger and purpose

&nbsp; \* A face in window glass that vanishes from memory the moment she tries to focus

&nbsp; \* Migraine so severe it feels like her skull might split

&nbsp; \* Carolina's worried voice pulling her back to her body



\#### 5\\. EXIT (What drives POV character forward):



The vision leaves Thea with specific details—systematic hunting, personal investment, deliberate methodology—that she can't explain knowing. Despite fearing she's having a breakdown, these details are too specific and organized to dismiss as random hallucination. She must investigate what she saw while hiding her "symptoms" from Carolina and the department, driven by the desperate need to prove she's not losing her mind.









\### CHAPTER 02



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------



\#### SCENE PLAN FOR CHAPTER 2



\#### POV: Kyrian



\#### POV CHARACTER EXPERIENCE:



Kyrian closes in on his prey through the Winter Court's crystalline forests, his Frozen Hunt pack flowing like liquid shadow around him. The traitor who sold Winter Court secrets to Summer dies as Kyrian intended—slowly, beautifully, frozen from the inside out while begging forgiveness. As the kill-high fades and he calls his hounds to heel, something impossible happens: the mate bond, silent and dead for four centuries, suddenly blazes to life in his chest like molten iron. The sensation is so powerful it drops him to his knees in the snow, gasping. Someone, somewhere, has awakened his mate's power. Tallow, his closest friend among the Hunt hounds, materializes beside him with worried eyes, but Kyrian can barely hear his warnings about letting sleeping bonds lie. All Kyrian can think about is her—the fae woman who rejected destiny itself, who chose a mortal over eternity, who disappeared twenty years ago leaving him with nothing but four centuries of carefully cultivated rage. She's alive. She's using power. And now he's going to find her.



\#### 1\\. THE CHANGE (What shifts for the POV character):



The mate bond Kyrian believed was permanently severed suddenly roars back to life, confirming his rejected mate is alive and actively using her power somewhere in the mortal realm.



\#### 2\\. WHY IT MATTERS (To the POV character):



This bond was Kyrian's greatest humiliation—publicly rejected by his destined mate in favor of a mortal. For four centuries, he's built his identity around being the discarded prince, the weapon shaped by rejection. Her power awakening means she's vulnerable to being found, giving him the first real chance at revenge he's had since she vanished. It also threatens to unravel the careful emotional control he's spent centuries building.



\#### 4\\. THE SCENE:



\* \*\*Opens:\*\* Kyrian standing over his kill in the Winter Court's crystal forest, ice-blue eyes fading back from hunt-black as the execution high subsides

\* \*\*Location:\*\* Winter Court's Whispering Woods, where traitors are traditionally hunted

\* \*\*Time:\*\* Deep Winter Court night, just after a successful hunt

\* \*\*Key Moments (as Kyrian experiences them):\*\*



&nbsp; \* The satisfying crack of ice spreading through the traitor's veins as justice is served

&nbsp; \* Sudden, violent awakening of the mate bond—like lightning in his chest

&nbsp; \* Dropping to his knees in shock, unable to process that she's alive

&nbsp; \* Tallow's concerned approach and warnings about "letting dead bonds rest"

&nbsp; \* The realization flooding through him: she's using power, which means she's findable

&nbsp; \* Cold fury replacing shock as he processes what this means

&nbsp; \* Decision crystallizing: he will hunt her as thoroughly as he hunted tonight's traitor



\#### 5\\. EXIT (What drives POV character forward):



The mate bond's awakening gives Kyrian something he's lacked for centuries: her exact location. She's no longer hidden, no longer protected by whatever magic concealed her. His obsession with revenge, fed by four hundred years of humiliation and rage, now has a clear target. He must find her, confront her, and finally claim what was meant to be his.









\### CHAPTER 03



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------



\#### SCENE PLAN FOR CHAPTER 3



\#### POV: Thea



\#### POV CHARACTER EXPERIENCE:



Thea arrives home to her converted Bridgeport warehouse after a grueling day at the precinct, her mind still haunted by Astrid's murder and the impossible vision she experienced. The grief for a stranger she's never met feels like a physical weight in her chest. Pouring herself a vodka, she considers going out to find someone to take home—anything to escape the crushing sense of loss she can't explain. Instead, she finds herself researching the frozen roses from the crime scene. Her academic background in ancient religions helps her navigate obscure botanical databases, where she discovers something impossible: the specific rose variety died out over two hundred years ago. Before she can process this discovery, her security alarm shrills through the warehouse. Someone has breached her carefully secured home. Gun in hand, Thea moves through her familiar space, which now feels violated and strange. Every shadow could hide an intruder, every sound makes her wonder if the vision was somehow connected to her personally. The fear that someone is specifically targeting her mingles with the terror that her "hallucination" might be affecting her judgment in a life-or-death situation.



\#### 1. THE CHANGE (What shifts for the POV character):



Thea discovers evidence that the frozen roses from the crime scene are from a species that's been extinct for two centuries, transforming her "impossible" vision from potential mental illness into something requiring a physical, real-world explanation.



\#### 2. WHY IT MATTERS (To the POV character):



This botanical impossibility validates Thea's decision to trust her vision rather than dismiss it as a breakdown, but it also means she's dealing with something beyond her understanding of reality. The discovery makes her feel safer mentally (she's not losing her mind) but far less safe physically (someone with access to extinct plants just murdered a woman, and now they've broken into her home). This directly threatens both her identity as a logical detective and her physical safety.



\#### 4. THE SCENE:



\- \*\*Opens:\*\* Thea unlocking her warehouse door, vodka bottle already in her thoughts as the weight of inexplicable grief presses against her chest

\- \*\*Location:\*\* Thea's converted Bridgeport warehouse - industrial space with case files scattered across tables, dying plants, and frost patterns on windows

\- \*\*Time:\*\* Late evening, approximately 9 PM after a long day at the precinct

\- \*\*Key Moments (as Thea experiences them):\*\*

&nbsp; - Pouring vodka while debating whether to hunt for a one-night stand to escape the crushing sadness

&nbsp; - Giving in to obsession and researching the roses instead of going out

&nbsp; - Finding the botanical database entry showing the roses died out in the 1820s

&nbsp; - Staring at her computer screen in disbelief, trying to process this impossibility

&nbsp; - The security alarm's piercing shriek cutting through her confusion

&nbsp; - Drawing her service weapon with muscle memory while her mind reels

&nbsp; - Moving through her familiar space that now feels hostile and invaded

&nbsp; - Fear mixing with validation—she's not crazy, but something impossible is happening



\#### 5. EXIT (What drives POV character forward):



The confirmation that the roses are physically impossible validates Thea's vision while simultaneously putting her in immediate physical danger. She must clear her home of intruders while processing that someone with access to extinct botanical specimens—someone potentially connected to her "hallucination"—may be specifically targeting her. This transforms her investigation from a professional obligation into a personal survival situation.

\#### SCENE PLAN FOR CHAPTER 4



\#### POV: Kyrian



\#### POV CHARACTER EXPERIENCE:

Kyrian waits in the shadows outside Thea's warehouse, ice-blue eyes fixed on the frost message he wrote on her windows—coordinates where she should have met him. Hours pass. She never shows. The familiar sting of rejection burns through him, stoking four centuries of rage into white-hot fury. When she finally returns home, she walks past his message without even glancing at it, as if he doesn't exist. Again. Just like she did four hundred years ago when she chose mortality over destiny. Fine. If she wants to continue pretending he doesn't matter, he'll force a confrontation. Breaking into her space feels wrong—uninvited entry violates ancient fae laws—but she's denied him everything else. The moment he steps inside, everything goes sideways. Her gun is trained on him before he can announce himself, and there's no recognition in those dark blue eyes. Not anger, not hatred, not even the cold dismissal he expected. Just professional wariness aimed at a complete stranger. When he speaks their old endearments, references their shared history, tells her about the mate bond, she stares at him like he's genuinely insane. The woman who consciously rejected him four centuries ago doesn't even remember him. Worse—the more he talks, the more she's clearly in agony. Her face contorts, she clutches her head, and when her eyes start bleeding silver, horror replaces his anger. This isn't willful ignorance. Something has stolen her memories entirely, and it's torturing her to even try to access them.



\#### 1. THE CHANGE (What shifts for the POV character):

Kyrian discovers that while Thea DID consciously reject him four centuries ago, she now has complete amnesia about him, their history, and even being fae—and trying to remember causes her excruciating physical pain.



\#### 2. WHY IT MATTERS (To the POV character):

\*\*This creates a devastating emotional paradox for Kyrian:\*\* she DID reject him consciously four centuries ago (validating his rage and humiliation), but now she doesn't even remember doing it. His carefully planned revenge required her to KNOW what she was losing, to remember why she deserved his wrath. Instead, he finds a blank slate who looks at him like a stranger. The woman who destroyed his understanding of destiny and love doesn't even know she did it. Worse, something has tortured her into forgetting her entire identity, making her a victim of forces beyond her control while he's spent centuries building his identity around being her victim.



\#### 4. THE SCENE:

\- \*\*Opens:\*\* Kyrian standing in winter shadows across from Thea's warehouse, watching frost-written coordinates on her windows that she's ignored for hours

\- \*\*Location:\*\* Outside and inside Thea's converted Bridgeport warehouse - industrial space that feels sterile and empty of any trace of the woman he knew

\- \*\*Time:\*\* Late evening, several hours after he wrote the message, just after Thea returns home from work

\- \*\*Key Moments (as Kyrian experiences them):\*\*

&nbsp; - Hours of waiting, watching her ignore his message as she enters her building

&nbsp; - The burning humiliation when she walks past his frost writing without even glancing at it

&nbsp; - Decision to break entry laws and confront her directly in her space

&nbsp; - Her gun aimed at him with no recognition in her eyes—she sees a dangerous stranger, not her mate

&nbsp; - Physical struggle for the weapon, noting her fighting skills but complete lack of personal knowledge

&nbsp; - Speaking their shared words, their history, watching her face show confusion rather than anger

&nbsp; - Her pain escalating as he talks—headaches, then silver bleeding from her eyes

&nbsp; - Realization that something magical is blocking her memories and torturing her

&nbsp; - Watching her lose consciousness from the agony of trying to remember

&nbsp; - Carrying her to bed while processing that the woman who chose to reject him has become someone else entirely



\#### 5. EXIT (What drives POV character forward):

The discovery that total amnesia has erased everything Thea was—including her conscious choice to reject him—forces Kyrian to completely rethink his approach. His revenge fantasy required her to remember her choice and suffer for it, but she's already suffering from forces beyond her control. Direct confrontation only tortures this stranger wearing his mate's face. He must find a way to understand what happened to her and whether the woman who rejected him still exists somewhere beneath this magical binding, or if she's truly gone forever.
