# NPE COMPLIANCE INSPECTION REPORT

## Executive Summary

**Overall Compliance Score:** ~85%

**Violations Found:**
- Critical: 3
- Moderate: 6
- Minor: 4

**General Assessment:** The outline demonstrates strong structural foundation and excellent character-driven causality. Major strengths include wound-driven decision-making, setup-payoff chains, and intimacy progression. Critical violations center on information economy (excessive off-page events), pacing mechanics (scene-end consequences not specified), and dialogue physics (insufficient attention to subtext requirements). The outline succeeds as high-level architecture but requires scene-level specificity in several areas.

---

## Violations by NPE Section

### Section 1: Causality & Plot Logic

**Violation 1: Inadequate Setup Distance for Major Reveals**
- NPE Rule: "Events require clear setup → event → consequence chains. Setup may be subtle but must exist minimum 2 scenes prior."
- Location: Ch 8-10, Thorne's betrayal reveal
- Issue: Thorne's deflection in Ch 5 and Ch 8 provides only 2-3 scenes setup before Ch 10 murder attempt. His specific willingness to order murder (vs. mere evasion) needs earlier behavioral foreshadowing.
- Severity: Moderate
- Recommendation: Add Ch 3-4 moment where Thorne demonstrates ends-justify-means thinking or prioritizes institutional reputation over individual safety. Needs earlier signal he's capable of lethal decision, not just information control.

**Violation 2: Coincidence Timing in Sarah's Supernatural Exposure**
- NPE Rule: "Coincidences increase pressure, worsen conflict, or reduce options. Never resolve problems or simplify paths."
- Location: Ch 21, Sarah witnesses glamour failure
- Issue: Timing feels precisely calibrated to create Act 2A climax rather than emerging organically. Why does glamour fail exactly when Sarah investigates exactly when Kira/Marcus need trust crisis?
- Severity: Moderate
- Recommendation: Establish mechanical cause for glamour failure (Veil instability from conspiracy's experiments, introduced Ch 15-18) so Sarah's investigation encounters weakened concealment. Makes timing causal, not coincidental.

**Strong Adherence: Investigation-Driven Plot Advancement**
- NPE Rule: "Investigation-driven plots advance through character initiative, never through convenient information delivery."
- Location: Ch 15-17 heist sequence, Ch 26 Conclave infiltration
- Why It Works: Characters actively risk, plan, and earn discoveries. No dropped clues or convenient informants—everything secured through character action with clear intention → obstacle → pivot structure.

### Section 2: Character Action & Decision Physics

**Violation 3: Insufficient Alternative Options Presented**
- NPE Rule: "Every major decision presents 2-3 plausible alternatives. Character chooses based on psychology, not plot convenience."
- Location: Ch 10 doorway choice, Ch 23 honesty confrontation
- Issue: Outline describes choice but doesn't specify plausible alternatives character considered. Ch 10: Could Kira report what she saw? Fake compliance while investigating? What makes helping Marcus the *only* psychologically viable option?
- Severity: Moderate
- Recommendation: For each major decision, explicitly list 2-3 alternatives character could plausibly choose and why psychology/wound makes them choose this specific path. Proves choice is character-driven, not plot-required.

**Violation 4: Cross's Motivation Shift Needs Earlier Setup**
- NPE Rule: "Decisions align with: Personal goals, Wounds, Blind spots, Biases, Relational tension, Emotional context"
- Location: Ch 28, Cross discovers he was conspiracy tool
- Issue: His wound (merit vs. advantage) established but his procedural obsession that leads to discovery needs more scenes showing him methodically investigating. One report in Ch 9 isn't sufficient buildup for Ch 28 pivot.
- Severity: Moderate
- Recommendation: Add 2-3 Cross POV moments (Ch 12, 16, 20) showing him compiling evidence, noticing inconsistencies, believing he's protecting institutional integrity. Makes Ch 28 discovery feel inevitable, not convenient.

**Strong Adherence: Protection-as-Harm Pattern**
- NPE Rule: "Trust issues must isolate characters from potential allies. Withholding information creates vulnerability that worsens outcomes."
- Location: Ch 19 (Marcus withholds), Ch 20 (Kira's secret contact), Ch 21 (Sarah endangered by Kira's lies)
- Why It Works: Every protection attempt backfires within promised 3-5 scenes. Causality is tight—Marcus's withholding nearly kills Kira, Kira's Thorne contact destroys trust, Sarah's ignorance creates danger. Demonstrates rule mechanically.

### Section 3: Dialogue Physics

**Violation 5: Dialogue Function Not Specified at Scene Level**
- NPE Rule: "Dialogue reveals emotion, creates tension, or shifts relationship dynamics. Cannot simply restate."
- Location: Throughout—scene descriptions don't specify dialogue mechanics
- Issue: Scenes list intention/obstacle/pivot but not how dialogue accomplishes these shifts. Ch 12 "Marcus explains conspiracy basics"—is this exposition dump or strategic persuasion? Ch 18 "examining evidence, ideological debate"—what subtext exists?
- Severity: Critical
- Recommendation: For key dialogue scenes (Ch 4, 12, 18, 23, 30), specify: (1) what's said directly, (2) what's withheld strategically, (3) what emotion dialogue reveals despite words, (4) how dynamics shift. Ensures dialogue serves dramatic function, not just information delivery.

**Violation 6: Intimate Confession Context Unclear**
- NPE Rule: "Intimate confessions occur only under duress or crisis pressure. Voluntary vulnerability is structurally rare."
- Location: Ch 33, final intimate scene before climax
- Issue: Description says "neither expecting to discuss feelings, but facing possible death." Duress present (mortality) but scene mechanics don't specify what forces confession despite resistance. What breaks through their analytical habit?
- Severity: Minor
- Recommendation: Specify triggering event—physical injury making mortality concrete? Separation required by plan forcing acknowledgment? Something structural that makes confession unavoidable despite preference for silence.

### Section 4: Pacing & Time Mechanics

**Violation 7: Scene-End Consequences Not Consistently Specified**
- NPE Rule: "Every scene ends with consequence altering: Options, Stakes, Emotion, Relationships, Power dynamics"
- Location: Multiple scenes throughout outline
- Issue: Many scenes list "consequence" but don't specify which of the five alteration categories occur. Ch 2 consequence: "first glimpse official narrative incomplete"—does this alter Kira's options? Her emotional state? Both? Ch 11 consequence doesn't specify power dynamic shift between Kira/Marcus despite status change (she's fugitive, he's guide).
- Severity: Critical
- Recommendation: For every scene, explicitly tag consequence with alteration type: "Options [reduced/expanded], Stakes [raised/lowered], Emotion [shifted from X to Y], Relationships [dynamic change], Power [who gained/lost]." Makes pacing mechanics trackable.

**Violation 8: Insufficient Intimacy-Crisis Sequencing Detail**
- NPE Rule: "Intimate scenes immediately followed by crisis, violence, or devastating revelation. Emotional safety never sustained beyond single scene."
- Location: Ch 25 (first intimacy), Ch 29 (second intimacy)
- Issue: Outline states scenes occur but doesn't specify what crisis/revelation immediately follows. Ch 25: "new vulnerability created" but what tests it next scene? Ch 29: Second intimate scene location in sequence unclear—what pressure follows?
- Severity: Moderate
- Recommendation: After each intimate scene, specify exact crisis that follows: Ch 25 → Ch 26 operation with lethal stakes tests new vulnerability. Ch 29 → Ch 30 Thorne confrontation forces choosing Marcus over mentor. Make pattern explicit.

**Strong Adherence: Investigation Scene Endings**
- NPE Rule: "Investigation scenes end with discovery that worsens situation or raises new questions."
- Location: Ch 6 (evidence complicates), Ch 17 (success escalates kill order), Ch 26 (learns timeline is days not weeks)
- Why It Works: Every investigative success creates new pressure. Never "got answer, problem simpler now"—always "got answer, situation worse than thought." Maintains tension escalation.

### Section 5: POV & Emotional Logic

**Violation 9: POV Character Selection Not Justified**

- Issue: Outline suggests Cross POV in Ch 28 but doesn't establish POV structure rules. Is this single-POV (Kira only) with Ch 28 exception? Dual-POV (Kira/Marcus)? Multi-POV? Each character's POV needs justification for when/why their perspective necessary.

- Why It Works: Cross's wound (merit obsession) makes him sympathetic despite harmful actions. Thorne's motivation (Rebecca's death gave meaning through Guard service) explains without excusing complicity. Both are protagonists of their own stories with comprehensible logic.

### Section 6: Information Economy

**Violation 10: Excessive Off-Page Event Summarization**
- NPE Rule: "No information appears unless character thinks, sees, learns, suspects, remembers, fears, or discovers it within story timeline."
- Location: Ch 31 (coalition building), Ch 38 (political aftermath)
- Issue: "Recruiting disillusioned Guard members" and "Conclave in crisis, conspiracy members facing justice" describe outcomes without specifying how character witnesses/learns these developments. Are these on-page scenes or summarized transitions?
- Severity: Critical
- Recommendation: Specify information access method. Ch 31: Show 1-2 recruitment conversations on-page (dramatize), reference others briefly through dialogue ("the three from archives agreed"). Ch 38: Show Kira witnessing one justice proceeding or hearing report from coalition member. No omniscient "here's what happened."

**Violation 11: Flashback Usage Not Specified**
- NPE Rule: "Flashbacks allowed only when shifting present-day stakes."
- Location: Ch 27, Marcus's past Guard operations revealed
- Issue: "Shows her classified files"—are these documents with photos? Marcus's direct narration of past? Flashback scenes? If flashback, what present-day stakes shift from seeing past vs. hearing about it?
- Severity: Moderate
- Recommendation: If flashback, specify what present revelation makes past matter NOW—e.g., Kira recognizes location from current conspiracy site, or Marcus's victim was someone important to current plot. If document review, specify what Kira sees that contradicts Marcus's self-description, forcing reckoning.

**Strong Adherence: Critical Reveal Timing**
- NPE Rule: "Critical reveals delayed until they create maximum impact and complication. Early revelation would simplify rather than complicate."
- Location: Ch 10 (murder attempt proves conspiracy), Ch 26 (timeline urgent), Ch 28 (Cross's complicity)
- Why It Works: Each reveal timed to maximize complication. Murder attempt after Kira's doubt accumulated (Ch 5-9 setup). Timeline revealed after partnership committed (urgency tests new trust). Cross's discovery after he's documented evidence (can't unsee truth). Perfect information economy.

### Section 7: Genre-Alignment Rules

**Violation 12: Memory Suppression Justification Incomplete**
- NPE Rule: "Memory loss as inciting incident only if: Suppression has mechanical cause (curse, spell, trauma), Attempting recovery risks death or worse, Lost memories directly connect to present threat"
- Location: Kira's backstory (13 years hallucinating before True-Sight manifested)
- Issue: Not technically memory loss but perceptual suppression. Outline doesn't specify if Kira's pre-True-Sight "hallucinations" were actual supernatural sight suppressed by trauma/magic or natural ability emergence. If suppression, needs mechanical cause.
- Severity: Minor
- Recommendation: Clarify if early chapter establishes Kira's sight was suppressed (by what mechanism?) or naturally latent. If suppressed, connect suppression to conspiracy (were they blocking True-Seers systematically?). Otherwise, reframe as natural ability emergence, not suppression.

**Strong Adherence: Power Systems Exact Cost**
- NPE Rule: "Power systems exact ongoing cost. Magic, supernatural abilities, or inherited traits require sacrifice, pain, or corruption."
- Location: Ch 7 (supernatural revelations require physical cost), Marcus's stolen artifacts throughout
- Why It Works: True-Sight established as draining/painful when used intensely. Artifacts Marcus stole contain "forbidden magic" implying cost. Conspiracy's Veil manipulation risks collapse with catastrophic consequences. Power never free—usage creates vulnerability.

**Strong Adherence: Romantic Intimacy Before Trust**
- NPE Rule: "Romantic intimacy occurs before emotional trust is earned. Physical connection precedes vulnerability and relationship security."
- Location: Ch 25 (first intimate scene at midpoint, before full trust)
- Why It Works: First intimate scene occurs after partnership commitment (Ch 23) but before deep trust tested and proven (Ch 26-29). Physical attraction acknowledged while emotional vulnerability still terrifying. Sequence is genre-compliant.

Actual scene would show realization through action/choice, not statement. Outline correctly separates architectural analysis from execution.
