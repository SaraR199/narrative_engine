# SECONDARY CHARACTER: Detective Sarah Nakamura

**Story Role:** Kira's former SDPD partner, current best friend (mundane human, unaware of supernatural world)

---

## 1. CORE IDENTITY & PSYCHOLOGY

**One-Sentence Identity:** A sharp-tongued detective whose loyalty drives her to protect friends even when it means investigating dangers she doesn't understand.

**Want vs. Need:**
- **Want:** To protect Kira from whatever danger she's hiding
- **Need:** To accept that love sometimes means trusting people's choices even when you can't understand them—control isn't protection

**Core Wound:** Three years ago, Sarah's previous partner was killed during an undercover operation Sarah planned. She missed a critical detail, and he walked into a trap. She carries guilt that her "incomplete investigation" cost a life.

**Coping Mechanism:** Using detective skills compulsively—if she can gather enough information, control enough variables, she can keep people safe. Information gathering creates the illusion of control over uncontrollable situations.

**Fatal Flaw:** Loyalty that overrides caution. Will investigate dangerous situations without backup if it means protecting someone she cares about. The very trait that makes her a good friend makes her reckless.

**Worldview:** "If I'm smart enough and persistent enough, I can prevent tragedy." Distorted by wound—she believes her previous failure was insufficient effort rather than accepting some things can't be controlled.

---

## 2. ARC STRUCTURE

**Arc Function:** Sarah's investigation creates escalating pressure through dramatic irony. Kira can't explain the truth without endangering Sarah, but withholding drives Sarah to dig deeper—demonstrating how "protective" lying damages relationships (Secondary Theme 1). Sarah represents the human life Kira risks losing (Secondary Axis 1: Identity/Belonging).

### Version 1 (Acts 1-2a): Concerned Friend
**State:** Notices Kira pulling away—missed calls, vague explanations, exhaustion. Accepts surface excuses but files away inconsistencies. Concern without alarm.
**Key Behavior:** Casual check-ins that mask observation. "You look like hell. New boyfriend or new case?" Uses humor to probe without confrontation.
**Plot Integration:** Early scenes establish their bond and Sarah's perceptiveness, making later lies painful for Kira.

### Version 2 (Acts 2b-3a): Active Investigator
**State:** Flimsy excuses pile up. Sarah's detective instincts override social boundaries—starts digging into Kira's absences, discovers connection to Marcus Chen (wanted fugitive), feels betrayed by systematic lying.
**Key Behavior:** Runs background checks, follows Kira, stakes out locations. Confronts Kira with evidence: "You're lying to me. I know you're protecting someone. Is it the fugitive? Are you in trouble or are you helping him?"
**Emotional Shift:** Hurt → Determined → Angry. The lies feel like Sarah failed again—if Kira won't trust her, Sarah will gather information herself.
**Plot Integration:** Sarah's investigation creates ticking-clock pressure. If she contacts official channels or follows Kira to wrong location, she'll draw Guard attention or walk into supernatural danger.

### Version 3 (Acts 3b-Resolution): Liability/Asset
**State:** Sarah's amateur investigation forces crisis. Either: (a) She follows Kira into supernatural confrontation, witnessing magic and requiring rescue, making her a liability, OR (b) Her mundane detective work uncovers a conspiracy connection Kira missed, making her an unexpected asset—but now she knows too much.
**Key Decision Point:** Kira must choose between continuing to lie (losing the friendship) or trusting Sarah with partial truth (risking her safety).
**Resolution:** Sarah demonstrates growth—accepts she can't control Kira's choices, offers support without demanding full explanation. "I don't understand what you're involved in. But I trust you. Call me when you can." Learns to trust instead of control.

---

## 3. VOICE & BEHAVIOR

**Speech Patterns:**
- Short, declarative sentences reflecting detective mindset
- Sarcasm deflects vulnerability: "Great talk. Very illuminating. Your lying skills could use work."
- Asks pointed questions disguised as casual conversation
- Swears when emotional guard drops: "Jesus, Kira, I thought you were dead."

**Emotional Expression:**
- **Concern:** Forced casualness masking observation. Brings coffee, lingers, watches body language.
- **Anger (feeling betrayed):** Cold precision. Presents evidence like interrogation. "You left the apartment at 2 AM. Your phone pinged near the waterfront. Care to explain?"
- **Fear (for Kira's safety):** Humor fails, voice tightens. Direct and vulnerable. "Whatever you're into, let me help. I can't lose another partner."

**Sample Dialogue:**

*V1 - Casual probing:*
"You missed poker night. Again. Jenny thinks you're seeing someone. I think you're working off-books. Which is it?"

*V2 - Confronting lies:*
"Stop. I've been a detective for eight years. You're not 'fine.' You're lying, you're exhausted, and you're connected to Seattle's most wanted. So either you're in trouble, or you ARE the trouble. Which?"

*V3 - Offering trust without understanding:*
"I don't know what's happening. I don't understand why you can't tell me. But I know you, and you don't do things without reason. So I'm trusting that. Just... don't die, okay?"

---

## 4. RELATIONSHIP DYNAMICS

**With Kira:**
- Shared history: ex-partners who chose friendship over professional distance after Sarah made detective
- Sarah knows Kira's tells, notices inconsistencies in cover stories
- Dynamic shifts from easy banter → tension → potential rupture as lies accumulate
- Sarah represents normal human life Kira risks losing by choosing supernatural world

**Function in Story:**
- **Emotional anchor:** Reminds Kira what she's sacrificing (simple human connection)
- **Pressure mechanism:** Investigation creates time pressure and stakes
- **Thematic embodiment:** Demonstrates how protective withholding destroys trust (Secondary Theme 1)

---

## 5. PLOT INTEGRATION

**Key Scenes:**
1. **Act 1:** Establishes bond through banter, shows Sarah's perceptiveness
2. **Act 2a:** Sarah notices Kira's absences, accepts weak excuses with visible doubt
3. **Act 2b:** Confrontation with evidence of Kira's connection to Marcus—"Are you protecting him?"
4. **Act 3a:** Sarah's investigation inadvertently creates danger (Guard notices her digging, or she follows Kira into supernatural situation)
5. **Climax proximity:** Kira must choose: continue lying or trust Sarah with partial truth

**Complications Created:**
- Dramatic irony: Reader knows supernatural truth Sarah doesn't
- Kira can't explain without endangering Sarah
- Sarah's loyalty makes her investigate despite warnings
- Her mundane investigation could expose supernatural world or draw Guard attention

---

## 6. NPE COMPLIANCE NOTES

**Protective Withholding → Isolation (NPE Rule 5):** Kira's lies to "protect" Sarah drive Sarah to investigate alone, creating the danger Kira tried to prevent. Demonstrates rule that withholding information to protect someone always backfires.

**Loyalty Inversion (Character Design):** Sarah's greatest strength (loyalty) becomes her fatal flaw when it drives reckless investigation. Mirrors NPE principle that virtues create vulnerabilities.

**Information Gathering Illusion:** Sarah believes enough information = control. Story tests this—some situations can't be controlled through knowledge. Must learn trust rather than control.

**Dramatic Irony Engine:** Sarah's ignorance of supernatural world creates tension every scene. Reader knows stakes she doesn't, making her investigation both sympathetic and terrifying.
