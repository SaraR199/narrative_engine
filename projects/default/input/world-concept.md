## What to Include

### Setting Basics
- When: Time period, era, date
- Where: Location, geography, scale (single room? planet? multiverse?)
- Reality type: Contemporary, historical, fantasy, sci-fi, magical realism, etc.

### World Rules
- What are the fundamental laws of this world?
- What's possible and impossible here?
- Are there magic systems, technology, supernatural elements?
- What constraints govern this reality?

### Social/Cultural Landscape
- What societies, cultures, or groups exist?
- What power structures are in play?
- What's the status quo?
- What tensions or conflicts exist at the societal level?

### Atmosphere & Texture
- How should this world FEEL?
- What's the sensory signature (gritty, lush, sterile, vibrant)?
- What mood does the setting create?

### Unique Elements
- What makes this world distinct?
- What will readers remember about this setting?
- What world-specific details matter to the story?

### World's Relationship to Story
- How does the world create or amplify conflict?
- What does the setting make possible?
- What does it make difficult or impossible?
